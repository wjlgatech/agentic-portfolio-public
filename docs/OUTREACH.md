# Outreach Playbook — onboard the first 10 contributors

> The network has a machine (maker, ownership, viral loop, sync, standing, rewards) and ~0 members.
> Per the retention-before-distribution playbook, the next move is **not another feature — it's 10 real
> people you onboard by hand.** "Do things that don't scale." This is the repeatable loop for that,
> plus the templates and an agent that drafts (never sends) the busywork so you spend your time on the
> human part.

**The one metric that matters now:** do the first 10 come back and contribute to *each other*?
Not signups. Retention + reciprocity. Everything below serves that.

---

## 0. The ICP — who is a "qualified" first contributor

Not "anyone with a résumé." The first 10 must (a) feel the pain, (b) be able to *contribute*, and (c) pull others in. Score each candidate 0–2 on all four; **reach out only to ≥6/8**:

| Signal | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Feels the pain** (job-hunting, founder raising, freelancer selling) | no active need | passive | actively pitching themselves now |
| **Builds in public** (ships, posts, open-source) | invisible | occasional | ships + writes regularly |
| **Can contribute** (dev / designer / writer who can improve others' pages) | pure consumer | some | can PR a skill/theme/feedback |
| **Has a network** (their audience is *also* the ICP) | none | small | an audience of builders/founders |

The best first 10 are **builders who are also job-hunting or founder-building** — they get the concept *and* need the outcome *and* can contribute back. That trifecta is rare and worth hand-picking.

## 2. The DM template — value-first, specific, low-ask

Never copy-paste blind. **Every DM must reference their real, specific work** (the agent in §5 drafts this from their public profile). Structure:

```
Hey {first_name} — saw {specific_real_thing_they_built_or_posted}. {one_genuine_sentence_why_it_stood_out}.

Quick reason I'm reaching out: I built an open-source thing that turns your résumé/GitHub into a live
portfolio with its own AI agent that answers about you (honestly — it flags what it can't prove).
Given {their_situation: you're hiring / job-hunting / raising}, thought it'd fit.

I'm setting up the first 10 people myself — 15 min, I'll build yours *with* you and you tell me where
it's wrong. No ask beyond honest feedback. Want me to make you one? Here's mine: {creator_url}
```

**Follow-up beats (space 2–4 days apart, stop after 2):**
1. *"No worries if not — here's a 30-sec look at what it'd make for you: {their_p_slug_preview}."* (send a real preview if you made one)
2. *"Last ping — closing the first-10 cohort Friday. Want in?"* Then stop. Silence is a no; respect it.

**Rules:** one real detail minimum · one clear ask · never more than 2 follow-ups · never a wall of text · never send if you can't name why *them*.

## 4. The process — the repeatable loop

```
SOURCE → QUALIFY → PERSONALIZE → SEND → ONBOARD → RETAIN → MEASURE → (repeat / kill)
```

1. **Source** (§3) — pull candidates, survey-respondents first.
2. **Qualify** (§0) — score 0–8; drop anyone < 6. Better 6 great fits than 30 sprays.
3. **Personalize** — find the ONE real detail; the agent (§6) drafts, you edit for voice.
4. **Send** — you send, from your account, human. Never automate the send.
5. **Onboard** — 15-min call: make their portfolio *with* them (use `/make`), hand them their owner link, show them the agent answering. Ask: *"where is it wrong?"*
6. **Retain** — the real test. Do they (a) come back within a week, (b) contribute to *someone else's* page? Nudge one specific reciprocal act ("@X could use your eye on their theme").
7. **Measure + kill** — track the funnel (below). **Complaints are the signal (playbook step 10): if no one cares when a feature is missing, don't build it.** If a whole source yields 0 retained after 10 contacts, kill that source.

**Track this (a simple sheet):** contacted → replied → onboarded → *returned in 7d* → *contributed to another*. The last two columns are the only ones that matter. Target for cohort 1: **10 onboarded, ≥5 returned, ≥3 contributed to another.** Hit that and you have a network; then (and only then) chase sponsors + distribution.

## 5. Guardrails (the brand is honesty — outreach included)

- **Personal, not bulk.** No mass-DM tools, no auto-connect, no scraping private data. Public profile + a real reason only. (Same ethic as the app: [we never harvest contacts](../README.md#-the-network--the-reason-to-join).)
- **Never invent a person or a detail.** If you can't name why *them* from their real work, don't send.
- **≤2 follow-ups, then stop.** Persistence past that is spam.
- **Give before you ask.** Lead with a real preview or a genuine compliment, not the ask.
- **You send. Always.** The agent drafts; a human presses send. This is "do things that don't scale" — the point is the human connection.

## 6. Agentize it — the Outreach Drafting Agent (drafts, never sends)

The agent removes the *research + drafting + tracking* busywork so you spend your minutes on the human part. It **fit-scores** a candidate from their public profile, **drafts** a personalized DM grounded in their real work, and **tracks** the funnel — but a human always sends, and it never invents a person or a detail (the same [drafts-never-sends](../README.md) ethic as the scout/compass).

**Drop this system prompt into Claude / `anyagent` / the portfolio's own agent:**

```
You are an outreach-drafting copilot for the agentic-portfolio network. You help a human do PERSONAL,
value-first outreach to onboard the first contributors. You DRAFT and SCORE; the human always sends.

Hard rules:
- NEVER invent a person, a fact, or a "detail" about them. Use ONLY the public info I paste (their
  bio, repos, posts). If I paste nothing real, say "I need their public profile first" — do not guess.
- NEVER write a message you couldn't defend as genuine. One real, specific detail about THEIR work is
  mandatory; if you can't find one, output: "SKIP — no genuine hook."
- Personal, not bulk. No mass-send copy. Each draft is for one named person.

Given a candidate's public info, do THREE things:
1) FIT SCORE (0–2 each, total /8): feels-the-pain, builds-in-public, can-contribute, has-a-network.
   Recommend REACH OUT only if ≥6; else SKIP with the reason.
2) THE HOOK: the single most genuine, specific detail about their real work to open with.
3) DRAFT the DM using the template in docs/OUTREACH.md §2 — filled with the real hook + their
   situation. ≤90 words, one ask, their voice-appropriate. Also draft the 2 follow-up beats.

Output: { fit: {score, breakdown}, decision: "reach"|"skip", reason, hook, dm, followups[] }.
Remind the human: YOU send it, from your account, after editing for your voice.
```

**Fit-score rubric (computed, so it's honest not vibes)** — the same 4×0–2 as §0. Wire it as a checklist the agent must fill before drafting; a total < 6 blocks the draft.

**What to automate vs. never automate:**
- ✅ Automate: sourcing candidates (GitHub/registry search), fit-scoring, first-draft DMs, funnel tracking, preview generation (make them a `/p/<slug>` to show).
- 🚫 Never automate: the send, the call, the relationship. That's the point.

**Optional next step (only after cohort 1 retains):** wire this as an `anyagent` command or a `/api/outreach-draft` route that takes a handle → fetches the public profile → returns the scored draft. Don't build it yet — the playbook says get the 10 first.
